Video Player Page

The page where users actually watch a teaching. Bound to /watch/[contentId]. YouTube iframe at top, curated sermon summary (never labeled "AI"), Scripture references with timestamp jumps, faith journey prompt, and related content recommendations. The single most important page for retention.

One sentence

The video player page is where TrueVi delivers on its value proposition — it's not just a video, it's a guided spiritual experience around a teaching. The player is YouTube; everything around it is TrueVi's proprietary layer.

YouTube TOS reminder (from CLAUDE.md)

The YouTube embed must remain freely playable. Never gate the player behind a paywall. Tier-gated value-adds live in the surrounding components (extended summary, scripture deep links, ScriptureViewer access) — never the video itself.

A guide to this teaching

Pastor Jane addresses the question that everyone wrestling with forgiveness eventually arrives at: is forgiveness a feeling or a decision? Her answer, drawn from Matthew 18, is that it is a decision — one we may have to make many times for the same wound.

She walks through the difference between forgiveness (releasing the debt) and reconciliation (rebuilding relationship), and is clear that the second is not always possible or safe. The teaching is especially relevant if you have been holding onto a wound that the other person is unwilling or unable to address directly.

Annotated walkthrough

1
Breadcrumbs · referrer-aware The breadcrumb chain reflects how the user arrived. From the Series page: Home › Ministries › Mercy Hill › Forgiveness Series › Episode 3. From a Subject page: Home › Subjects › Forgiveness › Episode 3. From a Search Response: Home › Answer · "How do I forgive" › Episode 3. Personalized navigation that respects the user's path.
2
Video Player · YouTube iframe + side meta The actual player is a YouTube embed (architectural decision — CLAUDE.md). Side panel surfaces per-user state: progress, save, share, transcript, tier-gated downloads. The video itself is always free; only the surrounding tools are tier-gated. The side panel collapses below the player on mobile.
3
Title bar Series context if applicable, video title in display type, pastor attribution, statistics. The "Subjects" tags link to subject collection pages (M3.6).
4
Curated sermon summary · the differentiator The TrueVi value-add above all others. AIResponseBlock with the "sermon-summary" tone. Includes a personalized framing ("This will be especially relevant if...") tuned to the user's inferred life events and faith stage. Timestamps are clickable — they seek the YouTube player to that point. Generation strategy: precomputed at content ingestion, NOT per-user per-render. Personalization is in the framing paragraph only, not the substantive content. Never labeled "AI."
5
Scripture references · extracted with timestamps Pulled from the sermon transcript at ingestion. Each reference shows the verse, translation, and a deep-link timestamp into the player. For TrueVi+ users, the reference itself opens the ScriptureViewer (deferred — see CLAUDE.md backlog · Bible API commercial license required first). Free-tier users see the verse but no expanded view.
6
Faith Journey prompt · the soul of the page A gentle, non-pushy invitation to engage. Appears conditionally: after watching ≥60% of the video OR on second visit OR if signals suggest emotional engagement (high dwell time, scripture references clicked). Copy avoids "AI" language — frames the moment as "is this speaking to where you are?" Save and Ask-a-follow-up are the two CTAs.
7
Related content · DYNAMIC slot For series videos, the first rail is "Continue the series" with remaining episodes. The second rail is Library-personalized cross-ministry content on the same subject. For non-series videos, the Library decides what rails to surface (typically one same-pastor rail and one cross-ministry subject rail).
8
Ask Anything · inline · context-prefilled The video's subjects pre-fill the AskAnything context. When the user submits, the Search Response can reference both their question AND the video they were just watching. Powerful for follow-up multi-turn conversations.

Curated sermon summary · how it's generated

This is the most technically involved part of the page. The summary is not regenerated per request. It's precomputed during content ingestion via the Content Library pipeline (referenced in CLAUDE.md):

// During content ingestion (CLAUDE.md content-library architecture)
1. Video is added to TrueVi catalog (from YouTube channel ingestion or manual)
2. YouTube transcript downloaded
3. Library processes transcript:
   a) Generates curated summary (~400 chars) — single canonical version
   b) Extracts scripture references with timestamps
   c) Extracts subjects/tags (already part of existing CMS pipeline)
   d) Generates 5 outline timestamps for navigation
4. Pastoral Board review (theological guardrails)
5. Published into content_items table

// At render time on the Video Player page
1. Renderer requests /library/compose-page
2. Library loads content's curatedSummary, scriptureRefs, outlineTimestamps
3. Library generates a *personalized framing paragraph* (~100 chars)
   based on the user's inferred faith stage and life events
4. Renderer composes: framing + curated summary + timestamps + scripture refs

Why precomputed

Per-render AI generation for every video view would be expensive and slow. Once-at-ingestion generation gives users instant page loads. The only per-user generation is the brief framing sentence — <100 chars, cheap, can be cached by inferred-stage cohort.

Scripture timestamp deep-linking

Both the summary timestamps and the scripture references include clickable timestamps that seek the YouTube player. The mechanic:

// User clicks a timestamp like "09:48"

1. Renderer calls YouTube iframe API: player.seekTo(588, true)
2. Player jumps to second 588 of the video
3. Emits "timestamp_jumped" event with { contentId, fromTimestamp, toSec }
4. Library logs this as engagement signal — boosts content's
   "navigated-to-scripture" score for personalization

This pattern works for any timestamp in any TrueVi UI — the outline timestamps in the summary, the scripture reference timestamps, and (future) discussion guide section headings.

Anonymous user behavior

Free browsing requires no signup (YouTube TOS, CLAUDE.md). Anonymous users get:

  • Full video playback — no degradation
  • Curated summary visible — basic free-tier version
  • Watch progress NOT persisted — refresh resets to start
  • Save button → "Sign up to save" prompt
  • Share works — share URL is just the canonical content URL
  • Faith Journey prompt appears but with copy: "Sign up to track where you are"
  • Related content rails — personalized by anonymous behavioral cookie

When the anonymous user signs up, their behavioral history (tagged with anonymousId) merges into their userId via the identity merge protocol (M1.1 §12).

Auto-advance behavior

When the YouTube player fires video_completed:

  1. If video is part of a series AND there's a next episode AND autoplayNextInSeries = true, the "up next" overlay appears (shown above).
  2. Countdown ticks 8 seconds. User can:
    • Click Play next — navigate immediately to next episode
    • Wait — navigate automatically at 0s
    • Click Watch later — overlay dismisses, page stays
  3. If video is NOT in a series, the related content rail scrolls into view and the journey prompt becomes prominent.
  4. For the FINAL episode of a series, the overlay shows: "Series complete. Here's what comes next:" with a CTABlock to a recommended next series.

Empty & edge states

Video not found

404 with a fallback page showing similar content based on the URL slug if extractable.

Curated summary not yet generated

Summary slot renders a placeholder: "A guide to this teaching is being prepared. Watch and we'll have it ready next time." Editorial team is notified. Page is otherwise fully functional.

No scripture references found

Scripture refs slot is hidden. Pastoral review queue notified that the video may have missed a tagging pass.

Content tier-gated AND user is below required tier

Player renders but shows a tier-gate overlay BEFORE playback: "This teaching is part of TrueVi Vine. Start a free 14-day trial to watch." — never blocks free content, only the gated subset.

Video pulled by ministry permission revocation

404 with: "This teaching is no longer available on TrueVi. Here are similar teachings from the same ministry:" + ContentRail.

YouTube iframe fails to load

Player area shows: "Unable to load video. Watch on YouTube directly:" + link. All other slots continue to render normally.
